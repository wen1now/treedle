Treedle

This is a laymen's explanation of Treedle. For a computer sciencey/mathematical explanation, click here.

Suppose you had to use the exact same strategy every time you played Wordle. So, for example, your first guess always has to be the same.

Treedle takes this to the extreme, by giving you every possible position you might find yourself in, and asking what you would do. In fact, it makes you map out your entire game of Wordle.

In other words, when the game ends, you will have created a set of instructions for playing a game of Wordle, of the form,

"if you have made these guesses and see so-and-so colors, then your next guess should be: [insert word]."

Your score, then, is the average number of guesses your strategy requires, multiplied by the total number of words in Wordle.
